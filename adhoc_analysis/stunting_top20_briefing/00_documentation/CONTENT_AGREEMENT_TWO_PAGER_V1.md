## Questions the Brief Must Answer

1. Where is stunting prevalence highest today, and which countries carry the
   greatest burden by number of children affected?
2. How concentrated is the global stunting burden across a small number of
   countries?
3. Which countries have made the greatest progress in reducing stunting
   prevalence over 20 years?
4. Which countries have achieved the largest reductions in number of stunted
   children, and how concentrated is that progress?
5. What methodological cautions should leadership keep in mind when interpreting
   these rankings?

## Methods and Limitations to Surface

- JME modeled stunting (HAZ < −2 SD) is appropriate for trend comparison but
  produces smoothed estimates, not direct annual observations
- Acute shocks (conflict, crisis) may not be captured in real time


## Claims to Avoid

- Causal explanations for why specific countries improved or did not
- Programme recommendations presented as findings
- Statements implying operational priorities not supported by the data
