## Draft For 1-Page Brief

### Executive Summary

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">Modelled JME estimates for 2024 show that very high stunting prevalence remains concentrated in a group of countries led by Burundi at 55.3 per cent, followed by Niger at 48.3 per cent, Eritrea at 48.0 per cent, Angola at 47.7 per cent, and Papua New Guinea at 47.6 per cent.</del>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">Modelled JME estimates for 2024 showed that very high stunting prevalence was concentrated in a group of countries headed by Burundi at 55.3 per cent, followed by Niger at 48.3 per cent, Eritrea at 48.0 per cent, Angola at 47.7 per cent, and Papua New Guinea at 47.6 per cent.</ins>

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">The burden profile is different</del> <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">The burden profile was different</ins>: India <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">had</ins> by far the largest estimated number of stunted children at 37.4 million,
followed by Nigeria at 11.4 million, Pakistan at 10.7 million, the Democratic
Republic of the Congo at 8.7 million, and Ethiopia at 6.9 million.

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">This suggests that leadership should distinguish</del> <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">This indicated that leadership needed to distinguish</ins> between countries where stunting intensity <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">was</ins>
extreme and countries where the absolute number of affected children <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">was</ins> driven
by population scale as well as prevalence.

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">Progress was visible but uneven.</ins> Over 2014-2024, the largest prevalence
reductions were recorded in Libya at 13.9 percentage points, Comoros at
11.1 percentage points, and Nepal at 11.0 percentage points. Over 2004-2024,
the largest reductions were recorded in North Korea at 27.6 percentage points,
Nepal at 25.3 percentage points, and Tajikistan at 24.9 percentage points.
<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">On the burden side, the largest 10-year reductions in the number of stunted children were recorded in India at 12.6 million, China at 3.6 million, Indonesia at 2.9 million, Pakistan at 2.4 million, and Bangladesh at 1.3 million. Over 2004-2024, the largest reductions were recorded in India at 27.5 million, China at 9.5 million, Bangladesh at 5.0 million, Indonesia at 3.9 million, and the Philippines at 1.3 million.</ins>

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">At the same time, high burden remains concentrated in very populous countries: the top five burden countries account for 75.2 million of the 115.3 million children represented across the top 20 burden countries in the current output.</del>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">The current 2024 number series summed to 149.2 million stunted children across the 162-country comparison universe used in this output. The top five burden countries accounted for 75.2 million of those children, or 50.4 per cent, and the top 20 burden countries accounted for 77.3 per cent.</ins>

### Key Messages

- <del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">The prevalence ranking and burden ranking should be read together rather than treated as interchangeable views of the problem.</del>
- A relatively small set of populous countries <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">carried</ins> most of the burden,
  even though the highest-prevalence countries <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">were</ins> partly different.
- <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">Reductions in the number of stunted children were possible at substantial scale, with India, China, Indonesia, Pakistan, and Bangladesh recording the largest 10-year burden declines in the current output.</ins>
- <del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">Some countries have achieved large declines in prevalence over 10-year and 20-year windows, but those rankings do not by themselves explain whether the number of affected children fell at the same pace.</del>
- <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">The fastest observed prevalence reductions were equivalent to about 1.4 percentage points per year, suggesting that sustained annual declines of around 1 percentage point had been achievable in some settings represented in this dataset.</ins>
- <del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">The current prepared input supports 162-country comparison universes for the latest-year, 10-year, and 20-year rankings used here.</del>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">Child stunting had to be viewed through both severity and scale.</ins> The
highest-prevalence countries <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">identified</ins> where modeled stunting rates remained

### Section 1 - <del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">What The Current Data Show</del> <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">What The Current Data Showed</ins>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">This briefing used modelled national stunting estimates for children under 5. The latest year available in the prepared input was 2024. On prevalence, Burundi, Niger, Eritrea, Angola, and Papua New Guinea ranked highest in the current output.</ins>

On burden, the picture <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">shifted</ins> sharply toward <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">larger-</ins>population countries,
with India, Nigeria, Pakistan, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and
Ethiopia accounting for the largest estimated numbers of stunted children.

### Section 2 - Where Progress Was Strongest

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">The strongest 10-year prevalence reductions in the current output are Libya, Comoros, and Nepal. The strongest 20-year reductions are North Korea, Nepal, and Tajikistan. These results show that large absolute declines are present in the series, but the ranking is endpoint-based and should be interpreted as a comparison of two time points rather than a full trajectory assessment.</del>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">The strongest 10-year prevalence reductions were recorded in Libya, Comoros, and Nepal, with declines of 13.9, 11.1, and 11.0 percentage points, respectively. The strongest 20-year reductions were recorded in North Korea, Nepal, and Tajikistan, with declines of 27.6, 25.3, and 24.9 percentage points. At the upper end of the observed distribution, these results implied an average annual pace of about 1.4 percentage points per year.</ins>

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">Burden change also matters. India records the largest reduction in the estimated number of stunted children over both 2014-2024 and 2004-2024, yet it still remains the country with the largest burden in 2024. Pakistan and Nigeria also remain among the highest-burden countries in 2024 even though both appear in the burden-reduction rankings. This is a useful warning against assuming that falling prevalence automatically removes countries from the highest-burden group.</del>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">Progress in the number of stunted children also warranted attention. India recorded the largest reduction in the estimated number of stunted children over both 2014-2024 and 2004-2024, yet it still remained the country with the largest burden in 2024. Over 2014-2024, the largest burden reductions were seen in India, China, Indonesia, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. Over 2004-2024, the largest burden reductions were seen in India, China, Bangladesh, Indonesia, and the Philippines.</ins>

### Section 3 - <del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">What Should Be Said Carefully</del> <ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">Data Considerations</ins>

<del style="color:#b91c1c; background-color:#fee2e2; text-decoration:line-through;">The current results support careful synthesis, not causal claims. They support statements about relative ranking, overlap, concentration, and measured change over the selected windows. They do not yet support stronger statements about why some countries improved more than others, whether conflict or other shocks fully explain current patterns, or how much residual burden reflects child population growth unless an explicit decomposition is added.</del>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">The modeled series was suitable for trend interpretation, but it was smoothed and should not have been read as a direct year-by-year record of change. Rapid shocks, including conflict and war, may not have been reflected immediately in the modeled series because data were often unavailable from those contexts.</ins>

<ins style="color:#166534; background-color:#dcfce7; text-decoration:none; font-weight:600;">The ranking universes in the prepared inputs included 162 countries for the latest-year, 10-year, and 20-year comparisons used here.</ins>
